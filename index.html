Statistics

About This Project

Chicago Population Explorer models the city's hour-by-hour population distribution across census blocks, revealing how the city's 2.7 million residents mix with over 3 million daytime workers and visitors.

Data Sources

  • US Census 2020 — Block-level residential population
  • CTA Rail Hourly Ridership — Chicago Data Portal (public)
  • US Census LEHD — Daytime population normalization
  • CTA GTFS — Station locations

Methodology

Overnight populations come from the 2020 Census at the block level. Hourly net inflows and outflows are estimated from CTA rail turnstile data, scaled by Chicago's transit mode share (~30%), and dispersed to census blocks within 400 meters of each station. Final estimates are normalized against LEHD daytime population benchmarks.

Tech Stack

  • Mapbox GL JS — Map rendering
  • D3.js — Charts and data binding
  • Python + GeoPandas — Data pipeline
